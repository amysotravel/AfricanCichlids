GETTING STARTED


Owning a fish tank is probably the most rewarding hobby I have experienced but also the most overwhelming to get started. I thought I would share some of my knowledge and experience to help my fellow fish tank enthusiast.


My experience comes from owning African Cichlids. They are probably the most colorful and hardiest of the freshwater breeds. It's the closest to salwater fish you can get without the heavy maintanence of a saltwater tank.


step 1: choosing your tank


Once you have purchased your fish tank you will want to cycle it for a few days. Cycling your tank is the process of bad bacteria converting to good baceria. This will result in a balance. It was probably the hardest news for me to hear when I started my tank and I'll admit I did not follow it the first time around. After setting up over a dozen tanks since then I realized that cycleing your tank really does save you massive headaches. You have less ammonia spikes and happier fish from the start. Some people say cycle for a week but I think 2 to 3 days is sufficient especially if you have all the essentials in place like good filtration, a de-chlorinator and heating.

ESSENTIAL ITEMS

These are my favorite products that I have tested and used for my fish tank aquariums.

Filtration System

A good filtration system is THE #1 essential for a healthy fish tank. Marinland is one of my favorite brands for fish tank supplies. This filter is inexpensive and makes my water look crystal clear within a few hours of getting it going.

Additional Filtration

Since I can't stress the importance of filtration enough it's probably no suprise that I use 2 forms of filtration. I like to have at least 1 or 2 sponge filters in my tank that not only help clean the water but also adds some oxygen with the extra bubbles.

Food

I have tried various cichlid foods for my fish but I have found this combination to be the most well rounded and tasty for my guys.

Aquarium Salt

Aquarium salt is different than Marine salt. I use it because it works as a conditionar and helps fight off any diseases like the dreaded ick. Since I started using Aquarium salt I have not had any ick breakouts.

Water Change System

I highly recomend getting a system like this to clean your water. After a few months of carrying buckets and spilling on the floor I made this little investement and never looked back.

LED Light

There are a lot of elaborate and fancy lighting systems out there but this one makes my fish look great and won't break the bank. The lighting that you use does not affect the health of your fish so I would go cheap to start out and save up for something better later on, once your fishies are all settled in.

Water Dechlorinator

You will need a water dechlorinator for a healthy fish tank. I tried many different dechlorniators until I came across this one. This is now the only one I use. My fish have had a much longer life span since switching to this product.

Heater

This is also a necessary product for your fishes happieness. I have purchased this heater multiple times when setting up new tanks. ALWAYS remember to turn the heater off when you change the water.

Extras

These are a few items I couldn't live without but are not essential for starting your tank. Having feeder with a timer has come in handy when I've gone out of town. The glass cleaner makes life so much easier when trying to clean up your tank. The Melafix is suggested to be good for your fishes external health (i.e. eyes, scales etc.). It has ingredients similar to essential oils. I wouldn't say it's a requirement but I use it often and am pleased with how my guys are looking.

Additional Food Options

There are a lot of great food options out there so in addition to the ones I recommend above I wanted to mention a few other good ones I have tried. As long as there is a high protien content you can't really go wrong.
